Common phishing signs
Urgent account warning

Messages that say your account will be closed today often try to trigger panic clicks.

Odd domain

Extra subdomains, lookalike spelling, punycode, or random words can indicate a cloned site.

Password ask

Never trust a link asking you to enter passwords, OTPs, or recovery codes under pressure.

Brand mismatch

Legitimate brands usually send people to well-known official domains rather than unfamiliar hosts.

What can the tool inspect?

The pasted URL, surrounding email text, extracted domains, and the wording used to push you into signing in.

Does it use a backend check?

Yes. If a live provider is configured, the tool can request server-side domain reputation data.
