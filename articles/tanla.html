Tanla Platforms Ltd — Fundamental Analysis

At just 12x earnings, Tanla Platforms stands out as the most attractively valued company in this set — a profitable, cash-generative CPaaS leader with 35% domestic market share, a debt-free balance sheet, and an AI-native platform showing international momentum.

Business Overview

Hyderabad-based Tanla Platforms Ltd (NSE: TANLA) is India's largest CPaaS (Communications Platform as a Service) company. It processes over 800 billion interactions annually and handles approximately 63% of India's A2P (Application-to-Person) SMS traffic. It also holds a 45% share in the domestic National Long Distance (NLD) business.

Its flagship product, Wisely AI, is an AI-native platform powered by NVIDIA GPUs that delivers real-time spam and scam protection. Tanla deployed Wisely AI in Indonesia in 2025 to tackle a $5 billion fraud problem with a leading telco — a meaningful signal of international traction. In early 2026, the company appointed a Chief Growth Officer for Asia and the Middle East.

Investment Thesis

Tanla's Q3 FY26 was a watershed quarter — revenue crossed ₹1,100 crore for the first time, marking its first double-digit revenue growth in six consecutive quarters. Management attributed this to strong SMS OTP volumes driven by rising digital transactions, new enterprise client ramp-ups, and growing omnichannel revenue. The enterprise segment grew 12.7% YoY alone.

The valuation case is unusually compelling for a technology company. Tanla trades at just 12x earnings against an Indian software sector average of ~25x and global CPaaS peers at 20–30x. A PEG ratio of 0.32 signals the stock is trading well below its growth-adjusted fair value. With a debt-free balance sheet, ₹457 crore in net cash, and 27%+ ROCE, this is not a speculative recovery story — it is a neglected quality business with a visible catalyst in Wisely AI's international expansion.

STRENGTHS

  • P/E of 12x vs sector average of 25x
  • 35% market share in India's A2P SMS
  • Debt-free with ₹457 Cr net cash
  • Wisely AI opens international growth vector
  • Record Q3 FY26 revenue — growth resuming

RISKS

  • OTT platforms gradually eroding SMS usage
  • Regulatory risk in telecom messaging
  • Sep 2025: revenue declined 24% (TRAI disruption)
  • International expansion unproven at scale
  • Small cap — higher price volatility
⬡ Analyst Verdict

Tanla is the standout value pick in this set. A dominant market position, clean balance sheet, improving quarterly momentum, and a P/E of just 12x combine into one of the better risk-reward setups in Indian tech. Q3 FY26's recovery — record revenue and first double-digit growth in six quarters — suggests the worst of regulatory disruptions is behind the company. Analyst consensus places the 12-month price target more than 20% above current levels, with DCF-based intrinsic value ranging from ₹460 to ₹835 per share. Suitable for a 2–3 year holding horizon with high conviction.
